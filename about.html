A hamburger is a sandwich made with a patty of ground beef that's been seasoned with salt and pepper, shaped into a round, and grilled or fried until it's cooked through.

The patty is usually served on a soft bun along with toppings like lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, cheese, and condiments like ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise.

The hamburger's origin can be traced back to the city of Hamburg, Germany, where beef was minced, seasoned, and formed into patties.

Immigrants brought this dish to the United States in the 19th century, and it became popular in fast-food chains like McDonald's and Burger King.
